Aloe Vera/ಅಲೋವೇರಾ (Aloe barbadensis miller)

CategorySucculent / Medicinal herb
Date of Planting-
Max Height60–100 cm (about 2–3 feet)
OriginArabian Peninsula (now cultivated worldwide, especially in tropical and subtropical regions)
Water RequirementVery low – about 300–500 ml once a week (allow soil to dry completely between watering; in hot summers it may need twice a week)
Seasonal FloweringSpring to summer (yellow/orange tubular flowers)
Medicinal ValueGel used for skin burns, wounds, and moisturizing. Improves digestion, has anti-inflammatory properties, and supports immune health.
Quantitative DataLifespan: 5–12 years. Mature leaf contains ~15–20 ml gel. Oxygen: Provides moderate oxygen release, especially at night (CAM plant).
Location-
Additional InfoAloe vera can survive without soil — its gel-filled leaves store enough water to grow even in just rocks or sand. Read More
